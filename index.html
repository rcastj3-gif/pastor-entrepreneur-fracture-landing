The Pastor-Entrepreneur Fracture

In the church, you're credible by default. You're the pastor. People listen.

In the marketplace, you have zero credibility. You're just another person claiming to have answers.

Most pastors never bridge this gap. They try to bring church authority into a marketplace where church authority doesn't exist.

Fracture #1: Credibility Collapse

You're credible by default in the church. Zero credibility in the market. Most pastor-entrepreneurs don't know how to rebuild marketplace credibility.

Fracture #2: Message-Market Misalignment

Your message works perfectly in a pulpit. It's designed for believers seeking deeper truth. Not for cold prospects who don't know you and are skeptical.

Fracture #3: Authority Role Confusion

You don't know what role to play: Coach? Expert? Teacher? Consultant? You're trying to be all of them, confusing your prospects.

Why This Matters

Visible costs: Offers that don't gain traction. Sales pages that convert at 0.3%. Webinars where 60% no-show. Courses that launch with 3 buyers instead of 30.

Hidden costs: You lose faith in your message. You question your calling. You blame your funnel instead of the fracture underneath.

The fracture makes you invisible to the exact people who need what you have.
